Model of information-psychological operation in international conflicts

Existing models, methods, methods and technologies of psychological impact on conflict situations have quite clearly expressed cultural-civilizational and national-state characteristics. In general, all their diversity can be combined in the framework of four cultural-civilizational models (or directions): Anglo-Saxon (representatives of the United States, Great Britain and the countries of the British Commonwealth), East Asia (China, Vietnam, Thailand, Taiwan, Japan) German (Germany, France, Italy, Scandinavian countries) and the Middle East (the Islamic world: Arab countries, Iran, Pakistan, Turkey, Indonesia).

Representatives of the Anglo-Saxon direction, in accordance with the Protestant world view, view the conflict settlement as basically an opportunity to transform, transform the world under their own model of society, and this is the goal pursued by the majority of US-developed technologies of information and psychological conflict resolution. The problem of combating international terrorism is almost turning from political to religious, when the US president states that "every country should know that the war on terror for America is not just politics, but a real vow" [1]. By the way, the ideology of compulsory, inevitable and absolutely free export of democracy from the USA to all other countries that do not yet possess "true democratic values" is also built in accordance with the Protestant traditions and has a clear religious implication.

The Anglo-Saxon cultural-civilizational approach to the use of modern technologies of information-psychological influence on political conflicts is well demonstrated by the US peacekeeping activity, based on three basic concepts:

- "power pacification", i.e. the establishment of a principled preference for the use of armed force and other forceful methods of pressure (including methods of state terrorism) with a view to resolving political conflicts on the conditions of an arbitrator, i. - the USA itself;

- "soft power" or "export of democracy", i.e. on the use of methods of compulsory transformation of the political picture of the world to their own standards and the massive application of information and psychological technologies for a "non-violent" change in state power ("velvet revolutions") in countries that do not seek to voluntarily enter the wake of US foreign policy;

- "unipolar world", i.e. the concept of the formation of a new world order with a political orientation to lower the role, ousting from the international political process and substitution in the processes of settlement of contemporary political conflicts of international organizations called to maintain security and stability in international relations - the UN and the OSCE.

All three concepts that underlie US peacekeeping activities are, in fact, classical psychological operations in which the use of direct armed force is seen as a service in relation to technologies of information-psychological management of the mass and individual consciousness of the population both directly in zones of international conflicts , and outside them. Recently, psychological operations of this nature in Russian studies are usually combined under the general name of "information-psychological wars" [2] .

Today, information and psychological warfare technologies are widely used by the United States in international conflicts, including Yugoslavia. [3], Afghanistan, Iraq, Somalia, Rwanda, in relations with Iran, and now in Kenya, where a pro-Russian united opposition came to power in the recent presidential elections. This is especially evident around the problem of the "international recognition of independence" of Kosovo, where the United States uses the entire available arsenal of psychological blackmail and information sabotage technologies, aimed at both the leadership of Serbia and the EU countries, to halt the decision to secede Kosovo from Serbia, half of which this step Albanian separatists do not support. That is why the very term "information-psychological warfare" (IPV) is of American origin and transferred to Russian soil from the dictionary of US military circles [4] .

The literal translation of this term ("information and psychological warfire") can sound both as an "information confrontation", and as an "informational, psychological war", depending on the context. The ambiguous interpretation of this term into Russian has caused the division of modern Russian scientists into two competing camps - the supporters of the "information confrontation" and the supporters of the "information war", despite the fact that, in the original language, this is essentially the same thing. Introducing the term "information-psychological warfare", American scientists, both civilian and military, adhere to the traditional for American culture pragmatic ideology, oriented to the near future: using the term "information war" they form the target in the minds of the ruling circles and the public at large, that in the future this form of relations will become so developed and effective that it will completely supplant the traditional armed confrontation. In American experts in the field of psychological operations, information war is used not so much as a term designating the current phase of development of conflict socio-political relations, but as a vector of foreign policy formation, as a program for choosing a political course and the ultimate goal of the evolution of instruments of political management. Therefore, the ongoing disputes over whether it is legitimate to call modern information and political conflicts information wars, or whether it is better to use the term "information confrontation" for this,

Within the framework of the Anglo-Saxon concept of information-psychological wars, actual combat plays a subordinate service role, and the plan for the armed campaign is built according to the rules and in accordance with the scenario of psychological impact on its citizens, on citizens of political allies and opponents and on the international community as a whole. This conclusion is confirmed by the US war in Iraq, which, with some changes in the political and military situation, in fact, continues to this day. It is no accident that former US Deputy Secretary of Defense John Nye points out that "the world in the Gulf saw the world through the eyes of CNN" [5] .

Just like the classic war, the information-psychological war consists of a sequence of military operations united by a single plan. Despite the rather large variety of types of information-psychological operations used in the modern political struggle, the planning and implementation of most of them is subject to the same logical scheme.

The standard operation of information-psychological warfare consists of the following sequence of actions (stages):

1. Political stratification of the international community.

2. Political polarization of strata playing a key role in the operation scenario.

3. Initialization of political activity of strata (in accordance with the role assigned to them by the operation scenario).

4. Controlled chain reaction.

5. Correction of the initial operation plans (feedback).

Let us dwell in more detail on the terminology used in describing the stages of the information-psychological operation. It should be noted that this basis of terms was specially developed by us to describe the field model of a psychological operation and is given in this paper for the first time.

1. A political stratum is a social association of politically active citizens who adhere to close political views and beliefs and strive to realize their rights and freedoms in the form of political activity both independently and jointly with other participants in the political process.

2. A political imperative is a rule of political behavior that is mandatory for all participants in the political community to fulfill in a given political situation. In this study, we will use this term in relation to the structural element of society - the political stratum.

3. Political stratification of society is the process of dividing society into social fractions (strata) under the influence of political ideology.

4. The political polarization of the stratum is the process of formation in the stratum of its own rules of political behavior (a system of political imperatives) that are mandatory for all members of the stratum.

5. The initialization of the political activity of strata is the result of the impact on the stratum of any external factor, the political reaction of the stratum to which is provided for at least one of its political imperatives.

6. Controlled chain reaction is a form of political activity of the stratum in the conditions of political polarization, in which at each stage of political situation change the political activity of the stratum is built in accordance with the next set of imperatives from the system of rules of political behavior formed in the process of its polarization.

7. Correction of the initial plans of the information-psychological operation is a form of feedback in the operation scenario, which provides for the evaluation of the effectiveness of the operation (comparison of the actual results of the operation with the expected operations) and the development of corrective amendments to the scenario of information-psychological impact.

In general, any information-psychological operation can be described as follows.

The information-psychological operation begins with the initiation of the process of dividing an as yet homogeneous society into fairly large politically homogeneous factions - political stratification. It should be noted that at the stage of political stratification, there is still no question of applying technologies for processing public consciousness: the goal of such stratification is the formation of target audiences and channels of political communication among these audiences, which will then transmit the information-psychological impact from one receptor to another. An example of such stratification is the "rallying" of a nation in the face of a threat, for example, a war - at the same time, all political camps are rallying: both supporters of the force decision and its opponents, and strict separation of some from others.

Suppose that the information-psychological operation is a form of implementing the political program of the aggressor state. In this case, the purpose of the information-psychological operation is to unite and mobilize the supporters of the political course of the aggressor and turn them into an obedient policy tool, on the other hand, to ensure voluntary submission to that part of society that may not accept the political ideology developed by the political strategists of the aggressor state , and, as a result, by their active actions, it is extremely difficult to implement the political course of this state in the sphere of international relations. To achieve this goal, it is necessary, first of all, to divide the society on a political basis into two or several camps:

- on a stratum that is generally ready to support the political course and consisting of both citizens of their country and citizens of other countries (supporters);

- on a stratum that is negative to the political course and ready to resist in its implementation (opponents);

- a stratum consisting of citizens who have not decided on their position, including politically passive; wavering, waiting and ready to join the majority;

- on the strata of sympathizers (potential allies).

As a result of this division, active social groups are formed in the society, nominating from their midst informal leaders around which the initial rallying of politically active citizens, adhering to generally close political views and beliefs. This process we will call political stratification, which forms a certain structure of the social environment in order to increase its susceptibility to the subsequent "point" manager of information and psychological effects. The division of society into strata on a political basis is the initial stage in the formation of centers of political attraction in the structure of society.

As a result of political stratification,

First, there is a division of society into target audiences (opponents, allies, waverers, etc.), each of which needs its own tactic of information-psychological treatment of public consciousness (this is important for the forces of operations of psychological warfare);

Secondly, informal opinion leaders are singled out from among the most active participants in the political process, for which IPT's policy technologists are developing an individual program for the processing of consciousness;

Thirdly, after dividing into several political factions, in each of the factions, outwardly quite spontaneous (and, in fact, chain) processes of socio-political self-organization begin: the political positions of individual members of the stratum "get attached" to each other and become more radical , categorical, polar. Doubts about the weightedness and reasonable sufficiency of the political convictions that the group adheres gradually disappear, and the collective opinion, which is always more simplified and categorical than the individual opinion, begins to prevail in assessing certain events. Thus, as a result of this process of self-organization, a collective consciousness is formed in the group, guided in the choice of its solution by a finite, relatively small number of factors,

The motivation for initiating the process of political stratification is the targeted ejection into the information space of a political ideology formed by the IPV forces that includes the image of a political event or process, its political assessment and a set of imperatives that regulate social relations in the special conditions created by this phenomenon in the political life of the nation, group of subjects of international politics or the world community as a whole. In the conditions of a lack of objective information from the places of events, the heterogeneity of information sources and the diversity of interpretation of the events that occur, the dominant participants in the political process, such an ideology provides a ready assessment of what is happening, in many cases already quite simplified, schematic and psychologically comfortable for the average person to perceive.

It should also be noted that in the event of a sharp rejection of the basic ideology by a person, its political imperatives are still partially imported into the mind, albeit with a psychological negation operator, leading to changes in the world's own picture and its adaptation to the threat that threatens these positions. In this case, a negative attitude towards the worldview imposed by the ideology also leads to activation of the processes of political stratification and, on the whole, contributes to the formation of target audiences and the establishment of communication channels between them, necessary for the transition from broadband information-psychological to "point-like".  

Formation of social factions on a political basis occurs if:

- the provisions of political ideology lead to the emergence or aggravation of social contradictions;

- the provisions of ideology introduce new or actual threats to its safe existence in society, including the threat to life;

- political ideology is supported by the state and does not allow a citizen of this state to evade defining their own political position with regard to its basic provisions;

- ideology invades the sphere of personal life and introduces into it cardinal changes, to which the citizen is not ready; while social adaptation to these changes is possible either in the form of active support for the ideological provisions, or in the form of resistance to its impact on one's own life, the lives of loved ones, friends and acquaintances.

In a politically stratified society, all members of the stratum adhere to close political views and believe that the division of society on a political basis is necessary to actively represent their interests in the political life of society.

In the social space, political strata form clusters and structures that are close in nature to the domain structures of ferromagnets: if we assume that the rules of political behavior in the stratum set a certain vector of political activity for each of its members, then within the boundaries of each stratum, each of its members leads itself coordinated with the vector of political activity of the entire political stratum as a whole. The vectors of political activity of different strata are differently directed and may both coincide and do not coincide with the vector set by the basic ideology that initiated the process of political stratification. In general, a stratified society, formed by a combination of political strata-domains, remains politically neutral:

As a result of stratification of society on a political basis, a set of politically active strata arises, which are used in information and psychological operations as objects of psychological treatment. It is obvious that those strata that in principle support the political course will become the main driving force of the psychological operation, the tip of that "ram" that will strike the disobedient and will become a convincing argument for those who doubt and hesitate. However, in order for this part of society to become an obedient tool in the hands of IPT technology strategists, it is necessary that it become manageable. For this, the next phase is in operation - the phase of applying the technology of political polarization of strata.

In order to make the political stratum manageable, and ideally an obedient tool for achieving the goals of the information-psychological operation, it is necessary that within the stratum a proper system of norms of political behavior be formed that is mandatory for unconditional implementation for all members of the stratum and ensures a given concerted political behavior Strata as a whole in the conditions of aggravation of the political situation. The formation of such a system of norms of political behavior occurs as a result of the impact on the stratum of the information-psychological warfare of an external ideology developed by political strategists. At the same time, in strata that unite potential sympathizers for the policy of the aggressor [6], the polarization effect takes place: the system of ideology of the individual members of the stratum is replaced by the provisions of the foreign political ideology developed by the aggressor, and the system of rules of political behavior adopted within the stratum and socially acceptable to all its members is replaced by a rigid and categorical system of political imperatives.

We will call this process political polarization. After its completion, the system of political imperatives of the basic ideology of the aggressor becomes for the stratum the only norm of political behavior, the "rules of the game" for any of its members, which makes such stratum an obedient tool for implementing the policy of the aggressor using the potential of the stratum "in the dark" in his political game.

As a rule, the provisions of the basic ideology are formed in such a way as to smooth out the consequences of the citizen's transferred stress from his "voluntary-compulsory" inclusion in the political stratum (the subconscious always very accurately recognizes and reacts to psychological coercion applied to a person) and, against such typically therapeutic influence, form in his mind a state of psychological comfort. In most cases, the resulting illusion of security and information superiority results in the voluntary replacement of his own assessments, conclusions and judgments (his picture of the world) in a person's mind with a ready-made set of provisions from political ideology. As a result, the political behavior of a person who has been adjusted to an ideological "umbrella" at any significant (from the point of view of the scenario of the psychological operation) changes in the political situation, it is built in accordance with the political imperatives contained in the basic ideology and becomes sufficiently predictable, and therefore manageable. When such citizens are united in the political stratum contained in the basic ideology, the set of political imperatives becomes norms of social behavior as a whole that extend not only to the sphere of political activity proper, but also to the whole spectrum of social relations within the stratum, and often becomes within this stratum new "rules" games".

So, for a person falling into a political stratum, a basic ideology is the rules of behavior in a political stratum that are binding for all members of the stratum. Penetrating into the consciousness of man in the early stages of political polarization as an exclusively external factor, ideology, ensuring the polarization of the political stratum, ceases to be an external program and becomes an internal norm of socio-political existence, an instruction on organized political behavior in accordance with the its new system of values ​​and political imperatives. We emphasize that the rules of behavior in the basic ideology are imperative in nature, which pursues two main goals:

a sufficiently high degree of independence of the political behavior of the strata from the conditions of the external environment;

programmability of stratum actions in conditions of arbitrarily changing political situation.

Indeed, the imperative is a schematically constructed instruction on the unconditional execution of a sequence of actions included in its text, a program that is triggered when the external initiation mechanism embodied in it is triggered. The imperative does not allow for the multivariate behavioral response to the same political situation; the actions of members of the political stratum, following the political imperatives introduced into their consciousness, are comparable with the functioning of modern electronic-mechanical systems. Thus, in relation to the individual, it is provided for its voluntary subordination and controllability of political behavior by the methods of hidden psychological coercion-without necessarily destroying or deforming its personal psychological personality.

As a reminder, it should be noted that similar systems of imperatives and the principles of building a basic ideology are used in modern Western totalitarian sects. However, the sects pursue the goal of completely subordinating the consciousness of adepts, while technologists of psychological operations are only interested in the controllability of political consciousness and behavior.

As an unconditional instruction to conduct under certain conditions of the political situation, the imperative in the process of maintaining group cohesion plays a significant separative and identifying role, drawing a clear line between "our" and "outsiders". "Their" know in advance how to act in this or that political situation and at the same time do not experience the problem of choice. This, in their opinion, is constantly supported by technologists of psychological operations, gives them an indisputable advantage over other categories of citizens and even raises them to a qualitatively new stage of evolutionary development. The remaining categories of citizens, unable to understand and accept the obvious, seem to belong to lower-organized biological organisms.

Returning to the role in the political polarization of the basic ideology, it should be noted that in modern psychological operations, only one basic ideology is rarely used, developed for a stratum providing the aggressor's political course with unconditional support; as a rule, similar ideological systems are developed for other strata that arise in the process of political stratification: for strata of successive or potential allies, for which only a minor modification of the basic ideology is needed, and for strata of overt and covert opponents against which special technology operates polarization effect.

For the opponents of the course of the aggressor, organized in their own strata, the process of political polarization is all connected with the same partial replacement of one's own values ​​with ideological positions and estimates imported from outside, which occurs in the following sequence of stages:

- Active resistance to the introduction into the consciousness of the provisions of the basic ideology leads not only to the manifestation of the true intentions of the aggressor (positive result), but also to the partial penetration into the consciousness of political opponents in it of the basic imperatives of the aggressor's ideology (negative result); penetration into consciousness occurs through the psychological negation operator, which changes the polarity of the positions of the basic ideology of the aggressor from negative to positive, without destroying the structure of the ideological constructions themselves;

- penetrating into the consciousness, the provisions of the basic ideology of the aggressor, supplemented by the negation operator, trigger in the human mind the mechanism of changing the world's own picture in order to adapt it to the threat that threatens these positions; for the aggressor it is important that at the same time, the conscious activity of political opponents in resisting the implementation of its political course should be built strictly in the basis of directions that are, in fact, prescribed in its basic ideology, i.e. the clash of interests and values ​​took place in a format defined by the aggressor's ideology (when the anti-ideology held by political opponents, in fact, repeats the neolinguistic structure of the ideology of the aggressor, only - with a reversed polarity);

- penetrating into the human subconscious, the provisions of the basic ideology of the aggressor, supplemented by the negation operator acting in the sphere of conscious mental activity, are released from this operator and, in fact, in the original form are fixed there.

Here there is a well-known physiological feature of the human psyche: the notion of "not" in the subconscious area of ​​the higher nervous activity of man does not exist, the negation operator does not work under these conditions: for example, if the child walking along the curb gives the command "do not fall," the probability its fall in this case increases many times. Thus, within the framework of such a scheme of psychological transfer of the positions of the aggressor's ideology through the consciousness of political opponents to their subconsciousness, only in consciousness these concepts are fixed in a negative context, with the negation operator, in the subconsciousness this operator disappears and the positions of the basic ideology are fixed as true.

Taking into account that only about 10% of the entire higher nervous activity of a person falls on its conscious part, and the rest - only the sphere of the subconscious, and knowing that the subconscious often blocks conscious activity based on the arguments of the mind, it can be argued that this technology of psychological influence, and, probably, several other technological schemes of manipulating the psychological state of political opponents as a whole are able, within the framework of modern operation, to ensure the controllability of cos and political opponents too.

Now, when the process of political polarization is basically completed, the information and psychological technologies of managing the vector of political activity of strata come into effect, which include the external initiation of political activity and the management of a chain psychological reaction that ensures the triggering of the stratum of the political behavior algorithm embedded in the individual and mass consciousness.

The political activity of the strata is regulated through two main mechanisms of psychological management:

- Firstly, these are sets of imperatives, introduced into the consciousness of members of the political stratum through the system of the basic ideology, each of which has its own mechanism of initiation (since the political imperative is a rule of political behavior that is binding for all participants in the political community under certain conditions political situation); such sets, as a rule, are few, and at the moment there is only one set in the political stratum, the others are inactive and are waiting for their external initiation;

- Secondly, this is a feature of the human psyche, based on the associativity of the external perception of the surrounding reality.

So, for example, it is known that if at the moment of finding a person in a certain psychological state (for example, in an excited state) touching him or shaking his hand, this action will be fixed in his subconscious and is uniquely connected with his psychological state at that moment in time i.e. a clear associative series will be formed; at repeated touch or repetition of the gesture from the side of the initiator in relation to this person, even if enough time has passed, the original causes of the excitation have disappeared and he is currently in a stable psychological state different from the previous one, the associative series will work and the person will return to the previous excited state, and the gesture itself will serve for this person by the external initiation of a chain psychological reaction. In psychology, on the basis of this feature of the human psyche, numerous techniques of "psychological anchoring" have been developed and are operating, in which emotional states recorded in the subconscious are called "anchors". In the human subconscious, the "anchors" are associated with external factors leading to their initiation, through an individual associative chain, by means of which it is possible at any time to return a person to the emotional state remembered by the subconscious and to change the psychological state and behavior of a person "by switching" from a stable state to a border state, and vice versa. These capabilities of modern "anchor" techniques create significant opportunities for the hidden, manipulative, control of human consciousness through its subconscious activities.

The same mechanisms of psychological management are used in the external initialization of political activity, and in the management of chain behavioral reactions of political strata, in accordance with the external program laid down in the basic ideology. As well as political imperatives, the key "anchor" associations are being introduced into the consciousness of members of the political stratum in the process of political polarization.

Moreover, the initial initiation of the program of political behavior of the stratum, as a rule, occurs from an external controlling impulse [7]; зато последующая цепная реакция и последовательное замещение, по мере их выработки и достижения промежуточных целей операции, одних групп политических императивов другими происходит, как правило, без внешнего участия, поскольку те промежуточные цели психологической операции, которые достигаются в результате деятельности страта, построенной в рамках одной группы императивов, в момент достижения этих целей становятся новым «спусковым крючком» — инициирующим механизмом, запускающим процесс автоматической замены прежней группы императивов, уже полностью отработанных, на новую из набора, содержащегося в базовой идеологии. Таким образом, в психологической операции конструируется не только цепочка последовательности психологических состояний политического страта, определяющих формат его деятельности на каждом из этапов психологической операции, но создается технологическая цепочка последовательной инициации этих состояний, основанная на ассоциативных техниках управления сознанием, в которой факторами инициации (запуска) каждого последующего состояния является результаты достижения целей, поставленных перед стратом при его переводе в предъидущее психологическое состояние.

Использование психологических «якорных» техник становится особенно опасным в условиях этнополитических конфликтов: многие разновидности массовых психологических состояний, сформировавшихся в течение исторического периода развития национального самосознания, включая — состояния пограничные и агрессивные, уже заложены в этнической памяти и, практически в неизменном виде, неосознанно (т.е. — на уровне коллективного подсознания) передаются из поколения в поколение. Их не нужно специально формировать под конкретную психологическую операцию. Исторические механизмы инициации этих состояний, не раз срабатывающие в исторической практике межнациональных конфликтов, также известны, т.е., для того, чтобы «переключить» население какого-либо этнического анклава из психологического состояния мирного добрососедства в состояние немотивированной агрессии, достаточно лишь незначительно адаптировать формат психологического импульса, инициирующего психологический механизм «переключения состояний», характерных для данного этноса, к современным условиям, — так, как это происходило в конфликте в Нагорном Карабахе, происходило и продолжает происходить в конфликтах на Балканах (Косово), и т.д.

И, наконец, в современных технологиях проведения психологических операций предусмотрен специальный механизм внешнего вмешательства в технологический процесс — это т.н. механизм «обратной связи», технология оперативной коррекции схемы операции в случае нарушения параметров технологической цепочки или возникновения непредвиденных ситуаций. Он отличается от описанных выше технологий обеспечения добровольной подчиняемости массового и индивидуального сознания тем, что, в сущности, это — механизм быстрого реагирования, когда корректировка вносится в условиях фактической нехватки времени и ресурсов. Многократно повышая эффективность психологических операций и увеличивая гибкость реагирования по отношению к любым, самым непредсказуемым, условиям меняющейся политической обстановки, он, месте с тем, вносит в технологический процесс дополнительные риски и элементы случайности: оценка эффективности операции по ее внешним проявлениям в среде, на которую направлено ее воздействие, всегда субъективна, а недостаток времени не позволяет в оперативном режиме набрать статистику наблюдений, необходимую для принятия единственно правильного решения. Существование этого субъективного фактора, наряду с явными преимуществами, создает для технологического процесса реализации психологической операции угрозу скрытого внешнего управления.

In conclusion, we note that the modern Anglo-Saxon model of information and psychological management of international conflicts sees the process of resolving conflicts in the complete, compulsory transformation of the political systems of the conflicting parties into their own political norms and standards (the so-called "democratic template"), using both methods of direct military pressure ("forceful pacification", "humanitarian intervention", the fight against "international terrorism", the concept of "hard power"), and the methods of indirect action (to the notion of "soft power", "velvet revolutions", psychological warfare). However, the policy of the United States based on this model, as it is being implemented, meets ever greater opposition. It is no accident that a well-known expert on US-China relations, E.P. Bazhanov points out,[8] . First of all, this concerns the countries of the East: Afghanistan, Iraq, where Washington's military actions are defeated, forcing the US to seek new approaches to the analysis and practical implementation of strategic doctrines. At the same time new trends and schools are born, new technologies of psychological influence on conflicts are proposed.
