Center for Computational Network Intelligence (CCNI)

We develop innovative solutions for challenging problems in application areas ranging from vehicle health and machine intelligence to emerging inter-connected human-machine and social systems.

We create computational theories, models and techniques that can enable us to better understand, characterize, anticipate, predict, intervene and control evolving events, behaviors, and phenomena in complex systems. We focus on transitioning our innovative technologies to products and platform for our owners and the DoD, IC and DHS communities.

We strive for robust and reliable research findings that are reproducible, replicable, and generalizable. We are committed to open science paradigm and actively collaborate with researchers and practitioners from universities and industries.

Research

Advanced Vehicle Health

Develop hybrid data-driven & model-based prognostics that fuse multiplex data to detect, attribute and predict impending faults for sustainable vehicle operations and missions.

  • HRL developed Next Generation Aircraft Prognostics technologies for Boeing platforms to exploit design engineering knowledge, filter/fuse weak signals, and overcome high-dimensionality in massive, heterogeneous operational data. These technologies use advanced machine learning algorithms to generate relevant and important features for early detection of impending faults, robust identification of root causes, and accurate prediction of component remaining useful life.
  • HRL developed Adaptive Prognostics technologies for GM platforms to automatically adapt existing physics-based prognostics models with new information provided by field data. These technologies enables the rapid deployment of prognostics models to similar, but critically different systems (e.g., new versions of existing systems) with optimal adaptations of existing prognostics models to changes in the system (e.g., aging parts) for accurate first-time detections of potential failure patterns in different operating conditions

Computational Social Intelligence

Develop anticipatory learning capability to understand and utilize hybrid social intelligence to forecast events, prevent surprise, and adapt to new norms with rigorous validations in scalable simulation and experimentation platforms.

HRL’s network alignment technology enables the identification and mapping of node, edge, and higher-order relations across two or more networks given limited, noisy initial correspondence. The video shows an example of aligning nodes from two networks via embedding and warping techniques.

Network Alignment Demo

Download Video: Closed Format: "MP4" Open Format: "OGG"

  • HRL’s Twitter geo-localization technology processes social networks (billions of edges and hundreds of millions of nodes) to accurately geo-locate more than 110 million users with a median error less than 7 km in ~80TB of Twitter data.
  • HRL’s network alignment technology aligns more than 21 million Twitter users to their alternative accounts on different social media platforms (Tumblr, WordPress, Facebook, Blogger, and Instagram) with an estimate of 95% accuracy.
  • HRL’s network inference technology leverages cognitive, sociological, behavioral principles with machine learning algorithms to harvest network-based and content-based features to classify group vs individual user accounts (98% accuracy), identify pro-ISIS accounts (90% precision and 99% specificity), and infer demographic attributes of social media user accounts (77% accuracy).

Intelligent Sensing

Develop active perceptual sensing and processing capabilities to contextualize, anticipate, and track rare and sudden changes in activity and behavior patterns for optimal, flexible, and timely information gain.

HRL’s SmarNet technology devises a self-organized intervention strategy to mitigate large-scale cascading failure by performing smart switching between alternate connections; Reduced the probability of large (>30 lines) failures (33% reduction for a cascade of 100 lines) on a random geometric graph of AC power grid with 4,096 nodes.

Collaborative Multi-Intelligence

Fuse OSINT and other INT to anticipate the spread, cascade and contagion behaviors of a target system to develop controlling strategies and curate dynamic sensors with optimized coverage and precision.
